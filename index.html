What's Project 21?

Our Mission

We design and initiate sustainable programs and projects aimed at solving challenges that affect the most vulnerable members of our society,women and our main areas of focus are:women empowerment,youth engagement and empowerment,food/agriculture/farming and WASH(Clean water and Proper sanitation)

Our Vision

To eradicate extreme poverty, food insecurity and create self-reliance among the most vulnerable members of our society,women,in Kakuma refugee camp.

Our Story

Project 21KE is a Youth initiative group founded by 5 UWC-Amala alumni from different nationalities,in Kakuma refugee camp on the 28th of February 2021. These students have commonalities in that they are passionate about giving back to their communities and transforming the livelihood of the vulnerable members of the society,women, in Kakuma refugee camp. The organisation was formed with the desire of making change in their community and later will extend it to the world. The organisationâ€™s main objective is to address needs-based problems mainly in the Kakuma refugee camp. The organisation believes in transforming the livelihood of the vulnerable members of society,women and creating a more sustainable future.

The name "Project 21" represents our focus on preparing refugees for the 21st century through relevant skills and education. We believe that with proper support, refugees can become productive contributors to their host communities and eventually their home countries when they are able to return.

Today, we operate multiple programs across education, vocational training, and basic needs assistance, supported by a network of international donors and volunteers.
